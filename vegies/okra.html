GARLIC

To plant and grow okra, follow these steps:

1. Choose a location with well-draining soil and full sun exposure. Okra prefers a soil pH between 6.0 and 6.8.

2. Plant the okra seeds about 1/2 inch deep, spaced about 12-18 inches apart, in rows that are about 2-3 feet apart. Alternatively, you can start the okra seeds indoors in pots and transplant them into the garden when they are about 4-6 inches tall.

3. Water the okra regularly, keeping the soil moist but not waterlogged. Mulch the soil around the okra plants to help retain moisture and suppress weeds.

4. Fertilize the okra plants with a balanced fertilizer, such as a 10-10-10 formula, according to the instructions on the packaging.

5. As the okra plants grow, weed around them regularly to prevent competition for nutrients and moisture.

6. When the okra plants are about 6-8 inches tall, start to hill them up by mounding soil around the base of the plants. This will help to keep the roots protected and prevent them from drying out.

7. Okra is ready to harvest when the pods are young and tender, usually when they are about 3-4 inches long. Use a sharp knife or scissors to cut the pods from the plant, taking care not to damage the plant.

8. Okra is best when it is fresh, so try to harvest the pods on a regular basis. Use them within a few days of picking, or store them in the refrigerator for up to a week.

With proper care, your okra plants should produce pods that are ready to harvest in about 2-3 months.
