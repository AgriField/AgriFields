BEETS

To plant and grow beets, follow these steps:

1. Choose a location in your garden that gets plenty of sunlight and has well-draining soil. Beets prefer cooler weather, so it's best to plant them in the early spring or late fall.

2. Before planting, prepare the soil by adding compost or well-rotted manure to improve its fertility and structure. Beets also require a lot of nitrogen, so you may need to add some fertilizer to the soil.

3. Sow the seeds about Â¼ inch deep and about 2 inches apart in rows that are 12 inches apart. Keep the soil moist but not waterlogged, and thin the seedlings to about 4 inches apart once they have grown to a few inches tall.

4. As the beet plants grow, keep the soil moist and weed regularly to prevent competition for nutrients and water.

5. When the beet roots are about the size of a golf ball, carefully thin them to about 4 inches apart to give the remaining plants room to grow.

6. When the beet roots are about the size of a tennis ball, they are ready to harvest. Gently dig them up with a garden fork, taking care not to damage the roots. Beets are best used fresh, but they can also be stored in the refrigerator for later use.

With proper care and attention, your beet plants should thrive and produce delicious roots that you can enjoy in a variety of dishes. Good luck with your gardening!
