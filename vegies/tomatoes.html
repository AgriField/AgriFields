TOMATOES

To plant and grow tomatoes, follow these steps:

1. Choose a location with well-draining soil and full sun exposure. Tomatoes prefer a soil pH between 6.0 and 7.0.

2. Plant the tomato seeds or seedlings about 1/4 inch deep, spaced about 18-24 inches apart, in rows that are about 3-4 feet apart.

3. Water the tomatoes regularly, keeping the soil moist but not waterlogged. Mulch the soil around the tomato plants to help retain moisture and suppress weeds.

4. Fertilize the tomato plants with a balanced fertilizer, such as a 10-10-10 formula, according to the instructions on the packaging.

5. As the tomato plants grow, weed around them regularly to prevent competition for nutrients and moisture.

6. Prune the tomato plants by removing any suckers that grow from the base of the plant, and any branches that are low on the plant and not producing fruit.

7. Support the tomato plants with stakes or cages to keep them upright and prevent the fruit from touching the ground.

8. Harvest the tomatoes when they are ripe, using a sharp knife or scissors to cut them from the vine. Ripe tomatoes will be firm but slightly yielding to gentle pressure.

With proper care, your tomato plants should produce fruit that is ready to harvest in about 3-4 months.
