GARLIC

To plant and grow garlic, follow these steps:

1. Choose a location with well-draining soil and full sun exposure. Garlic prefers a soil pH between 6.0 and 7.0.

2. Separate the garlic bulbs into individual cloves, and plant each clove about 1-2 inches deep, with the pointed end facing upwards. Space the cloves about 6-8 inches apart, in rows that are about 12-18 inches apart.

3. Water the garlic regularly, keeping the soil moist but not waterlogged. Mulch the soil around the garlic plants to help retain moisture and suppress weeds.

4. Fertilize the garlic plants with a balanced fertilizer, such as a 10-10-10 formula, according to the instructions on the packaging.

5. As the garlic plants grow, weed around them regularly to prevent competition for nutrients and moisture.

6. When the garlic plants are about 6-8 inches tall, start to hill them up by mounding soil around the base of the plants. This will help to keep the bulbs protected and prevent them from drying out.

7. Harvest the garlic bulbs when the leaves begin to turn yellow and start to fall over. Carefully dig up the bulbs, and brush off any excess soil.

8. Cure the garlic bulbs by allowing them to dry in a warm, well-ventilated location for about two weeks. This will help to improve their flavor and extend their shelf life.

With proper care, your garlic plants should produce bulbs that are ready to harvest in about 7-8 months.
