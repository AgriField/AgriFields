COTTONS

To plant and grow cotton, follow these steps:

1. Choose a location with well-draining soil and full sun exposure. Cotton prefers a soil pH between 6.0 and 7.0.

2. Plant the cotton seeds about 1/2 inch deep, spaced about 12-18 inches apart, in rows that are about 3-4 feet apart. Alternatively, you can start the cotton seeds indoors in pots and transplant them into the garden when they are about 6-8 inches tall.

3. Water the cotton regularly, keeping the soil moist but not waterlogged. Mulch the soil around the cotton plants to help retain moisture and suppress weeds.

4. Fertilize the cotton plants with a balanced fertilizer, such as a 10-10-10 formula, according to the instructions on the packaging.

5. As the cotton plants grow, weed around them regularly to prevent competition for nutrients and moisture.

6. When the cotton plants are about 12-18 inches tall, they will begin to produce flowers. The flowers will eventually turn into cotton bolls, which are the fruit of the cotton plant.

7. Monitor the cotton bolls closely, and harvest them when they are ripe. To harvest the cotton, use your fingers or a pair of scissors to carefully open the boll and remove the cotton fibers.

8. Dry the cotton fibers in a warm, well-ventilated location, and then use a cotton gin to remove the seeds and other impurities.

With proper care, your cotton plants should produce cotton bolls that are ready to harvest in about 4-5 months.
