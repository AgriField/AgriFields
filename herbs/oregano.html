OREGANO

To plant and grow oregano, follow these steps:

1. Choose a location in your garden that receives plenty of sunlight. Oregano prefers well-drained soil, so make sure the location you choose has good drainage.

2. Prepare the soil by digging a hole that is slightly larger than the oregano plantâ€™s root ball. Mix some compost or well-rotted manure into the soil to provide nutrients for the plant.

3. Place the oregano plant in the hole, making sure the top of the root ball is level with the surrounding soil. Fill in the hole with soil and gently pat it down.

4. Water the soil well to help the oregano plant settle into its new home.

5. Keep the soil moist but not waterlogged, and fertilize the oregano every few weeks with a balanced fertilizer to help it grow strong and healthy.

6. As the oregano grows, you may need to trim it to maintain its shape. Use sharp scissors or pruning shears to carefully cut back any long or overgrown branches.

Once the oregano is established, you can start using the leaves in your cooking. With proper care, your oregano plant will continue to grow and provide you with fresh herbs for many seasons.
